MRI Compatible Robotics

MRI-Guided Parallel Manipulator for Prostate Transperineal Interventions

(In Collaboration with Harvard Medical School, Johns Hopkins University, Queen’s University and Acoustic MedSystem, Inc.)
– Clinical-grade robotic sytsem for image-guided prostate cancer therapy, approved by IRB for clinical trials
– Ultrasonically actuated 4-DOF parallel manipulator
– Custom developed MRI-compatible robot controller

MRI-Guided Robotic Assistant for Precision Conformal Ablation of Brain Tumors

(In Collaboration with Albany Medical College, UMass Medical School and Acoustic MedSystem, Inc.)
– Designed 8-DOF MRI-compatible robotic manipulator for thermal ablation of brain tumors

Robotic System for MRI-Guided Stereotactic Neurosurgery

(In Collaboration with Albany Medical College and UMass Medical School)
– Developed 6-DOF robotic system for deep brian stimulation
– Evaluated MRI compatiblity of the system with 3T MR scanner
– Evaluated needle placement accuracy with optical tracking system and MRI phantom studies

Modeling of Asymmetric Tip Needle Steering

– Developed a novel continuous uncoupled rotation velocity independent (CURV) needle steering model
– 2D model evaluation with camera tracking
– 3D model evaluation with CT phantom studies

Concentric Tube Continuum Robot with Piezoelectric Actuation for MRI-guided Intervention

(In Collaboration with Vanderbilt University)
– Integrated the kinematics model of concentric tube continuum robot with MRI-compatible robotic system
– Targeting accuarcy evaluation with optical tracking system and MRI phantom studies

Fully Actuated Robotic Assistant for MRI-Guided Prostate Biopsy and Brachytherapy

(In Collaboration with Harvard Medical School)
– Developed fully actuated 6-DOF robotic system for prostate biopsy and brachytherapy
– Targeting accuarcy evaluation with optical tracking system and MRI phantom studies

Teleoperation System with Hybrid Pneumatic-Piezoelectric Actuation for MRI-Guided Needle Insertion with Haptic Feedback

– Integrated fiber optical force sensor with piezoelectrically actuated slave robot,based on Fabry-Perot interferometry (FPI) principle
– FEM analysis and optimization of sensor flexure
