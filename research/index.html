MRI Compatible Robotics

Enabling Technologies for Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI)-Guided Prostate Interventions

Project Summary
– Abstract: robotic system performing prostate biopsy under live MRI-guidance with manual insertion. Clinical trials approved by the Institutional Review Board (IRB). Clinical studies are currently ongoing at Brigham and Women’s Hospital
– System architecture: network-based modular design consisted of navigation software, robot control software, fiber optic interface, robot controller, robot manipulator
– Robot Design: 4-DOF parallel manipulator, manual insertion
– Controller: MRI compatible, lose noise, precise closed-loop control for ultrasonic motors, reliable safety mechanism
– NIH funded project, in collaboration with Harvard Medical School, Johns Hopkins University, Queen’s University and Acoustic MedSystem, Inc.

Responsibilities
– Developed and debugged the controller hardware of the 4-DOF robotic prostate biopsy system
– Implemented the kinematics model in robot controller with Java
– Conducted thorough pre-clinical evaluation in aspects of targeting accuracy and MRI compatibility
– Assisted clinicians performing successful prostate biopsy on 11 patients

Teleoperated Needle Placement System with Hybrid Pneumatic-Piezoelectric Actuation

Project Summary
– Abstract: fully actuated slave robot performing precise autonomous prostate biopsy and brachytherapy, which can also be controlled remotely with haptic master device
– Slave robot: 3-DOF Cartesian base, 3-DOF needle driver, integrated with Fabry-Perot interferometry (FPI) force sensor
– Master robot: 1-DOF linear translation, 1-DOF rotation, integrated with pneumatic haptic feedback
– Congressionally Directed Medical Research Project (CDMRP) funded project

Responsibilities
– Developed a fully actuated 6-DOF slave robot for prostate biopsy and brachytherapy with SolidWorks modeling and rapid prototype machining (laser cutter, 3D printer, lathes, millers)
– Evaluated the targeting accuracy of autonomous biopsy and brachytherapy with MRI phantom studies
– Designed a FPI force sensor flexure; utilized FEA (finite element analysis) to optimize the force distribution
– Integrated FPI force sensor into slave robot giving clinicians real-time tissue stiffness feedback

MRI-Guided Robotic System for Precision Conformal Ablation of Brain Tumors

Project Summary
– Abstract: MRI-compatible robotic system performing precise probe placement and thermal ablation dose delivery under real-time MRI-guidance
– System architecture: network-based modular design consisted of navigation software, robot control software, fiber optic interface, robot controller, robot manipulator
– Robot design: 3-DOF Cartesian base, 2-DOF orientation arm, 3-DOF ablator driver, 1-DOF head frame
– Controller: MRI compatible, lose noise, precise closed-loop control for piezomotors
– NIH funded project, in collaboration with Albany Medical College, UMass Medical School, Acoustic MedSystem, Inc.

Responsibilities
– Developed an 8-DOF fully piezoelectrically actuated robotic manipulator for brain tumor ablation
– Designed mechanisms using SolidWorks modeling and selected MRI & bio-compatible materials; machined, built and tested multiple prototypes
– Established kinematics model (plotted trajectory) with MATLAB simulation; wrote and tested the Java code for the robot controller
– Assembled and integrated system modules inside of a 3T MRI scanner and verified clinical workflow

Modeling and Control of a Gaussian-based Asymmetric Tip Needle Steering

Project Summary
– Abstract: a novel Gaussian-based needle steering model which can generate curved trajectories by controlling the needle insertion and rotation independently
– Robot: 3-DOF Cartesian base, 3-DOF needle driver
– In collaboration with University of Twente

Responsibilities
– Proposed a novel Gaussian-based needle steering model for asymmetric tip needle steering
– Evaluated modeling accuracy in 2D with camera tracking and 3D with CT phantom studies
– Implemented autonomous and teleoperated needle steering under real-time MRI-guidance based on Gaussian-based needle steering model

Steerable Needle Intervention with Real-time Feedback Using FBG-based Shape Sensing

Project Summary
– Abstract: Fiber Bragg grating (FBG) based shape sensing method that is able to reconstruct the needle shape and estimate tissue properties during steerable needle intervention under MRI guidance
– Robot: 3-DOF needle driver
– Research internship project at Johns Hopkins University

Responsibilities
– Developed real-time needle shape sensing and tissues properties estimation method based on sensors, which reconstructed visualization of the needle shape with polynomial curve fitting
– Implemented the FBG shape sensing based steerable needle intervention on a 3-DOF automatic needle driver, and validated with camera based phantom studies
– Implemented needle steering inside multi-layer tissue using FBG shape sensing
