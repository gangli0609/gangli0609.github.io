Automation and Interventional Medicine Robotics Lab, WPI, Worcester, Aug 2011 - Present

Research Assistant on multiple medical robotic R&D projects:

Clinical trial approved, Institutional Review Board, 2014.
– Developed PIC 32 embedded system-based controller hardware for 4-DOF robotic prostate biopsy system
– Conducted thorough pre-clinical evaluations; set-up robotic system at BWH and assisted clinicians performing successful prostate biopsy on 11 patients
– Proposed a novel Gaussian-based needle steering model; utilized MATLAB to develop simulation models on needle positioning; qualified targeting accuracy and validated with MRI phantom study

MRI-Guided Precision Conformal Ablation Therapy for Brain Tumors, NIH funded project, in collaboration with Albany Medical College, and UMass Medical School.

– Established kinematics model (plotted trajectory) with MATLAB simulation; wrote and tested the Java code for the robot controller
– Assembled and integrated system modules inside of a 3T MRI scanner and verified clinical workflow

Teleoperated Prostate Needle Placement System with Hybrid Pneumatic-Piezoelectric Actuation, a funded CDMRP (Congressionally Directed Medical Research Project).
– Developed a fully actuated 6-DOF slave robot for prostate biopsy and brachytherapy with SolidWorks modeling and rapid prototype machining (laser cutter, 3D printer, lathes, millers)
– Designed a FPI (Fabry-Perot interferometry) force sensor flexure; utilized FEA (finite element analysis) to optimize the force distribution
– Integrated FPI fiber optical force sensor into slave robot giving clinicians real-time tissue stiffness feedback

MRI-Guided Robotic System for Precision Deep Brain Stimulation Probe Placement

Concentric Tube Continuum Robot with Piezoelectric Actuation for MRI-guided Intervention
